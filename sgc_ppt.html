Problem Statement

The challenge SGC faces without a data-driven engagement system

SGC, a consulting firm seeking to bid on contracts across Massachusetts industries, currently relies on manual data lookup across thousands of vendor and company records — making strategic, resource-efficient decision-making nearly impossible.

Core Pain Points
  • No centralized view of which industry sectors hold the highest concentration of target companies for outreach
  • Vendor landscape is fragmented — medical, logistical, and professional providers tracked in separate static lists
  • SDO (Supplier Diversity & Opportunity) compliance data is unstructured, limiting CSR reporting capabilities
  • No visibility into federal contract spending patterns in Massachusetts that signal where SGC should focus bids

Project Objectives

Three guiding questions that shaped our analytical approach

01 — Market Density

Which industry sectors (Healthcare, Tech, Finance) show the highest concentration of SGC Target companies for immediate consulting outreach?

02 — Vendor Optimization

How can we visualize the vendor landscape to ensure a balanced mix of medical, logistical (VEH), and professional (PRF) service providers?

03 — Compliance & Diversity

What is the distribution of SDO Commitment Percentages across the vendor pool, and how does this impact SGC's CSR reporting?

High-Level Goal

Deliver a functional, data-driven dashboard that transforms static lists of thousands of companies and vendor contacts into an actionable Business Development Funnel — enabling SGC leadership to move from manual data lookup to strategic, educated decision-making regarding resource allocation and partnership strategy.

Data Sources & Dataset Overview

Primary datasets used to build the SGC Growth Intelligence Toolkit

Dataset 1 — Categorized Companies

  • 147 companies across 10 industry sectors
  • Sectors: Healthcare, Finance, Tech, Energy, Education & more
  • Includes national, local, and SGC-targeted classifications
  • 58 priority accounts flagged for immediate outreach

Dataset 2 — Vendor Contact Details

  • 1,621 vendors across 15 service categories
  • Service codes: MED, FAC, ITT, PRF, VEH & more
  • Includes contact names, roles, emails, phone numbers
  • 1,234 vendors with SDO commitment % data

Dataset 3 — USASpending.gov (Massachusetts)

  • Federal contract & grant obligations by NAICS code
  • Top 10 awarding agencies by spend in MA
  • HHS leads at $14.9B; Facilities Support tops NAICS at $19.5B

Slide 08

Key Insights & Strategic Findings

Data-driven takeaways that directly inform SGC's business development strategy
1
Finance & Healthcare dominate SGC's target landscape

Finance & Insurance (27 companies, 9 targets) and Healthcare & Biotech (21 companies, 9 targets) represent the highest-density opportunity sectors for immediate outreach.

2
Facilities is the strongest SDO performer

With an average SDO of 17.9% and Sustainable Facilities averaging 35.3%, this category offers SGC the most compelling diversity narrative for CSR-conscious clients.

3
54.8% of vendors fall below 5% SDO commitment

A significant diversity gap exists across the vendor pool — SGC can position itself as a diversity-forward consulting partner to address this gap in client supply chains.

4
MA federal spend signals high-value contract verticals

Facilities Support ($19.5B), Building Construction ($17.9B), and Aircraft Manufacturing ($12.9B) are the top NAICS categories — all serviceable through SGC's current vendor network.

5
HHS & SSA represent 77.7% of top agency spend

Healthcare-adjacent and social services consulting is the dominant federal investment category in MA — a direct strategic priority for SGC's government contract bidding.

6
IT sector holds highest-SDO individual vendors

NEWCOM Wireless (51%), Digit Outsource (46%), and CenturyLink (36%) are standout diversity-committed IT vendors SGC should prioritize in tech contract proposals.

Conclusion & Recommendations

What SGC should do next with the Growth Intelligence Toolkit
Strategic Recommendations
R1
Prioritize Finance & Healthcare outreach

Activate the 18 combined SGC targets across these two sectors immediately using the vendor contact funnel.

R2
Leverage SDO data in contract pitches

Use Facilities and Staffing SDO leaders as proof points when pitching CSR-aligned contracts to government and non-profit clients.

R3
Target HHS & SSA-funded contracts in MA

With $26.9B combined obligations, healthcare and social services federal contracts are SGC's highest-value bidding opportunity.

R4
Expand vendor SDO data coverage

387 vendors currently lack SDO data — enriching these records will strengthen SGC's diversity reporting and vendor selection process.

Conclusion

The SGC Growth Intelligence Toolkit successfully transforms thousands of unstructured company and vendor records into a five-tab interactive dashboard that answers SGC's three core business questions on market density, vendor optimization, and diversity compliance.

By integrating Massachusetts federal spending data from USASpending.gov, the toolkit goes beyond internal data to position SGC with a real-world contract bidding strategy grounded in $100B+ of publicly tracked federal obligations.
